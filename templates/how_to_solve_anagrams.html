How to Solve Anagrams

Practical techniques you can use without an online solver

1. Look for common prefixes and suffixes

Many English words share familiar endings like -ing, -ed, -tion, -ment, and -ly. Try grouping letters to see if any of these patterns emerge.

2. Identify vowels first

Vowels give structure to words. Count how many vowels you have and try placing them in likely positions. A long word with only one vowel greatly narrows the possibilities.

3. Rearrange physically

Writing letters on paper or tiles and physically moving them around can reveal patterns your brain may miss when looking at a fixed string of letters.

4. Think about word meaning and context

In crosswords, the clue definition often gives strong hints about the answer’s meaning, tense, or part of speech. Use that information to guide your rearranging.

5. Break longer anagrams into chunks

For longer anagrams, try splitting the letters into smaller groups and solving each part independently, then recombining them.

6. Practice common letter patterns

Experienced solvers recognize common letter groupings instantly. The more puzzles you solve, the faster these patterns become automatic.
